CADAgent

About CADAgent

CADAgent is a free, open-source add-in for Autodesk Fusion 360 that lets engineers and designers create parametric CAD geometry using natural language. Instead of navigating through menus and dialog boxes, you describe what you need in plain English, and CADAgent translates that into real Fusion 360 features.

The project

CADAgent was created by Erik Forsten, a software engineer who got tired of the disconnect between knowing exactly what geometry you want and the tedious process of clicking through CAD menus to get there. The idea was simple: if you can describe a part to a colleague and they'd understand it, why not describe it to your CAD tool?

The project is fully open source and available on GitHub. It launched as version 1.0 in 2025 and is actively maintained.

How it works

Under the hood, CADAgent uses Anthropic's Claude API to interpret your natural language descriptions and convert them into Fusion 360 API calls. This means the output is real parametric geometry — sketches, extrusions, fillets, chamfers, threads, and patterns — that appears in your Fusion 360 timeline and can be edited just like any manually created feature.

This is fundamentally different from AI tools that generate mesh files (STL/OBJ). Parametric features are editable, dimensionally constrained, and manufacturing-ready. You retain full control of your design.

Technology

  • AI: Anthropic Claude API (bring your own key)
  • Platform: Autodesk Fusion 360 (Windows and macOS)
  • Language: Python (Fusion 360 API)
  • License: Open source

What's next

A web-based version of CADAgent is in development, which will allow you to generate CAD geometry directly in the browser without needing Fusion 360 installed. Assembly support, more complex feature types, and support for additional CAD platforms are on the roadmap.
